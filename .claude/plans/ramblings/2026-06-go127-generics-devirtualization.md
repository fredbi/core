# Ramblings — does go1.27 devirtualize our generic lexer cores? (no)

> Research notes, 2026-06-25. Settles a question parked during the L/VL
> unification: the ~5% per-token cost of the policy-parameterized cores comes from
> a method call on a *type-parameter value* routing through the generics
> dictionary as an indirect call (Go does not devirtualize it). Would go1.27 —
> which also brings jsonv2/jsontext into the stdlib — change that? Measured against
> go1.26.4 with go1.27rc1 installed locally. Companion to
> [codegen-asm-jit](2026-06-codegen-asm-jit.md) and
> [perf-and-paradigm](2026-06-perf-and-paradigm.md).

## TL;DR

- **No devirtualization.** go1.27rc1 keeps the generics-dictionary indirection for
  type-param method calls. Confirmed at three levels: inliner diagnostics,
  disassembly, and runtime benchmarks. The ~5% dict-dispatch tax on `L` is
  unchanged; **don't wait for 1.27 to reclaim it.** Road (b) (the
  `refactor/inline` generator) remains the only lever if we ever want it back.
- **Two surprises** (both in 1.27rc1, may shift by GA):
  1. The **jsontext yardstick regressed 5–23%** under 1.27rc1 (same
     `go-json-experiment` source, so purely the compiler). Our lexer stayed flat,
     so our *relative* standing improved — not because we sped up.
  2. The **verbatim pull paths got 6–16% faster** under 1.27 — general codegen
     helping the heavier `verbatimPolicy.emit` (struct building), **not**
     devirtualization (the identity `semanticPolicy.emit` didn't move).

## Background — what the ~5% actually is

The unified cores are `scanTokenG[T, P emitPolicy[T]]` (pull) and
`scanPushG[T, P]` (push). Each token is emitted via `p.emit(...)`, where `P` is a
concrete zero-size policy (`semanticPolicy` → `token.T`, identity `return t`;
`verbatimPolicy` → `token.VT`, wraps + position). Because Go compiles a generic
function **once per GC-shape** (stenciling) and reaches the concrete method
through a runtime **dictionary**, even a one-line identity `emit` on a zero-size
type becomes an **indirect call through the dict** inside the core — not inlined,
not devirtualized. That per-token indirect call is the measured ~5% on `L`.

## Method — three levels of evidence

### 1. Inliner diagnostics (`go build -gcflags=-m=2`)

Identical under both toolchains:

```
generic.go:51:6: can inline semanticPolicy.emit with cost 2  ...
generic.go:60:6: can inline verbatimPolicy.emit with cost 23 ...
<autogenerated>:1: inlining call to semanticPolicy.emit
<autogenerated>:1: inlining call to verbatimPolicy.emit
```

Both `emit`s are inlinable, but in **both 1.26.4 and 1.27rc1** they inline only
into the `<autogenerated>` **dictionary shim** — never directly into
`scanTokenG`/`scanPushG`. No `devirtualizing` line appears under either.

### 2. Disassembly (`go tool objdump -s scanTokenG`)

Structurally byte-identical between the toolchains. At every emit site
(`generic.go:857–885`) both emit **indirect calls**:

```
generic.go:880   41ffd4   CALL R12     ; dictionary-loaded function pointer
generic.go:877   ffd0     CALL AX      ; indirect
... (same instructions, same lines, same count under 1.26.4 and 1.27rc1)
```

`CALL R12` / `CALL AX` = indirect dispatch. A devirtualized call would be a direct
`CALL <symbol>` (or no call at all, if inlined). It is not.

### 3. Runtime benchmarks (`benchstat`, count=10, Ryzen 7 5800X)

`BenchmarkLexers`, 5 workloads × the generic-core impls + jsontext yardstick.
Same machine, sequential runs (1.26 then 1.27). Raw outputs kept alongside this
note: `2026-06-go127-bench-go1.26.4.txt`, `2026-06-go127-bench-go1.27rc1.txt`,
`2026-06-go127-benchstat.txt`.

**Geomean: +0.54% slower (sec/op), −0.55% (B/s) under 1.27rc1. Allocs identical
everywhere.** No net win.

Generic **semantic** cores (the dict-dispatch carriers) — flat or *slower*, never
the ~5% drop a devirtualization would produce:

| workload | impl | 1.26.4 | 1.27rc1 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ints | default-lexer/bytes (pull) | 517µs | 517µs | ~ |
| ints | default-lexer/tokens (push) | 398µs | 438µs | +10.0% |
| ints | default-lexer/reset | 514µs | 534µs | +3.8% |
| canada | default-lexer/bytes | 566µs | 609µs | +7.6% |
| canada | default-lexer/tokens | 449µs | 494µs | +10.1% |
| citm | default-lexer/tokens | 1.96ms | 2.03ms | +3.7% |
| twitter | default-lexer/bytes | 974µs | 1008µs | +3.5% |

jsontext yardstick — regressed everywhere under 1.27rc1:

| workload | 1.26.4 | 1.27rc1 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| citm | 1.56ms | 1.93ms | +23.4% |
| strings_plain | 190µs | 232µs | +22.2% |
| ints | 347µs | 398µs | +14.5% |
| canada | 465µs | 496µs | +6.8% |
| twitter | 1.04ms | 1.09ms | +5.4% |

Verbatim **pull** paths — consistently *faster* (general codegen, not devirt):

| workload | 1.26.4 | 1.27rc1 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| ints | 2.47ms | 2.08ms | −16.0% (noisy base) |
| twitter | 2.92ms | 2.59ms | −11.2% |

| canada | 1.82ms | 1.67ms | −8.5% |

(The verbatim *push* path `lab/verbatim-tokens` did **not** share the win.)

## Why this isn't measurement drift

The 1.27 run followed the 1.26 run, so uniform thermal throttling would bias
*everything* slower. Instead the deltas are **directional**: semantic cores
slower, verbatim-pull faster, jsontext much slower — consistent across all five
workloads. Drift can't produce opposite signs; the effects are real (for this RC).

## Interpretation

- The semantic `emit` is identity: there is nothing to optimize *but* the
  dispatch, and the dispatch is unchanged → flat/slower. The verbatim `emit` does
  real work (`AsVerbatim` + `WithPosition` struct build) → benefits from 1.27's
  general codegen/escape-analysis, independent of the (still-present) dispatch.
  This split is itself evidence that **the dispatch did not change** — only the
  surrounding real work moved.
- 1.27rc1 looks net-neutral-to-slightly-negative for tight byte-scanning loops.
  It is an RC; some of this (and the jsontext regression) may be ironed out by GA.

## Implications

- **The ~5% is structural, full stop.** Confirmed at IR, machine, and runtime
  levels across two compiler generations. Accept it (decision already taken) or
  reach for the generator (road b). PGO does not help here either (it devirtualizes
  *interface* calls from profiles, not generic-dictionary calls).
- **For the planned json/v2 contrib lexer:** wrapping `encoding/json/jsontext`
  (stdlib as of 1.27) stays the clean plan, but **re-measure against the stdlib
  tokenizer at GA** — the experimental module's hot path regressed 5–23% under
  1.27rc1, and GA numbers are what users will actually run.
- **Re-test at go1.27 GA**: both the jsontext regression and the verbatim-pull win
  are RC observations; worth a quick re-run when the final lands.
